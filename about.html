I'm Yujia Si, a UX researcher, designer, and a developer who loves mindfulness meditatoin and journaling.

My journey into the realm of mental health technology began in 2020 when I joined Education Without Barriers (https://educationwithoutbarriers.org/), a non-profit organization. Initially, I was largely unaware of the profound mental health struggles faced by children who are economically disadvantaged, physically challenged, or otherwise hindered from accessing quality education. As a volunteer English teacher at EWB, I provided one-on-one sessions, and my student, Yangyang, a courageous child battling leukemia, introduced me to these struggles. Unable to attend regular school due to ongoing treatments and isolated from peers, Yangyang faced depression and anger, challenges that demanded I learn to teach strategically and patiently. This meaningful experience was not only an inspiration but also a critical turning point that sharpened my focus on the significant information gap in China around psychological disorders. It was through Yangyang's eyes that I truly understood the potential of technology to bridge these gaps.

Furthering my commitment, my college years were marked by engagement in diverse research projects, notably including usability testing for a sleep chatbot and a study exploring the effects of BTS music on mental health. These academic pursuits enriched my understanding and fueled my passion to innovate in the field of mental health technology.

The experiences, from the intimate teaching sessions at EWB to rigorous academic research, have ignited my passion to explore and contribute to mental health solutions through technology. As I look forward, I am eager to continue my research, aiming to leverage mental health technology in transformative ways to support those in need.
