I'm Yujia Si, a UX researcher, designer, and a developer who is passionate about mental health technology.

My path into mental health technology began in 2020 when I joined Education Without Barriers (EWB), a nonprofit that highlighted the intense mental health challenges faced by disadvantaged and physically challenged children. While teaching English at EWB, I met Yangrui, a resilient young student with leukemia. Her struggles with depression and isolation due to her inability to attend regular school deepened my understanding of the barriers faced by children like her and the role of technology in bridging these gaps.

Throughout college, I engaged in various research projects, including usability testing for a sleep chatbot and studying the impact of BTS music on mental health. These experiences have shaped my approach and fueled my passion to innovate in mental health technology, especially in the development of AI-powered chatbots and digital mental health tools.

Driven by these formative experiences, from personal encounters at EWB to academic research, I am committed to advancing mental health solutions through technology, aiming to make a meaningful impact on those who need it most.
